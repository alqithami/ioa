Internet of Agents

The First International Workshop on the Internet of Agents (IoA)

WI Conference Paper Submission

Omaha, Nebraska United States

October 13, 2016

IoA Workshop,


Submission Instructions and formatting:
  • The workshop will feature invited talks by leading researchers and an exciting collection of oral presentations as well as an interactive poster session.
  • There will be a nomination for an outstanding paper award which includes a free conference registration.
  • Submissions accepted in IoA will be allocated 4 pages in the workshop proceedings to be published by IEEE Computer Society Press and indexed by EI, and will be available at the workshops.
  • All submissions will be peer reviewed and evaluated by 2-5 program members and auxiliary reviewers on the basis of soundness, significance, presentation, understanding of the state of the art, and overall quality of their technical contribution.
  • We advice authors to refer to all the information and data that might be helpful to best evaluate their submissions (e.g., \footnote{URL} or \cite{} data used in your experiment).
  • The formatting style for IEEE computer society proceedings can be found at: Manuscript formatting guidelines.
  • Although we accept submissions in the form of PDF or DOC files, you are strongly encouraged to generate a PDF version for your paper before submitting it.
  • Articles including files containing software demos, videos, etc. are encouraged to notify us before the submission. In this case, your LaTeX file along with all the files required to execute your paper in a different device must be compressed (.zip) and ready to be submitted along with your paper by the time the camera-ready version is due.

  • When you are ready to submit your paper, please go to the submission website and follow the instructions:

    • The Paper Submission Website


Poster Instructions:
  • The conference is expected to provide boards and tape/push-pins. You are responsible for mounting your material on the board for presentation. The poster dimensions for each presenter will be 4x4 (i.e., 4 feet high by 4 feet wide). This task should be completed BEFORE the workshop openning.
  • A representative from the authors should remain by the poster board during the entire session for clarifications and to answer some questions. The session is short so that a good poster allows someone to grasp quickly what your research is about and allows you to explain your ideas to them in more detail in case they are interested. The session should be used as an opportunity to dig deeper into your work, and give attendees an opportunity to ask questions, so your presence is essential. Please retrieve your poster material promptly at the end of the session, or it will be discarded.
